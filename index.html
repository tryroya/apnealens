The study

What ApneaLens is

Published research has long suggested that craniofacial structure — the geometry of the face and jaw — is one of many factors associated with how people breathe during sleep. It's a promising idea, and promising ideas deserve rigor.

ApneaLens is Orma Sleep's effort to study that connection the right way: data collected fresh with informed consent under research protocols reviewed by an institutional review board (IRB), stored securely, analyzed offline by our research team, and compared against real reference measurements. Nothing is inferred about any individual, and nothing is reported back to participants.

What ApneaLens is not

Three things we want to be unmissably clear about.

01

It does not diagnose, screen for, detect, or assess the risk of sleep apnea or any other medical condition.

02

It is not a medical device, and it returns no health results to participants.

03

It is not a substitute for care. If you're concerned about your sleep, talk to a healthcare professional.

Our approach

Why we work this way

Orma Sleep builds sleep technology under a simple rule: measure first, claim later. Before any tool can responsibly say anything about sleep, it has to earn that right through validated evidence. ApneaLens is that work in progress — done in the open, with participants as partners.
